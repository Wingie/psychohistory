## What this is

A position paper arguing a bounded form of Asimov's psychohistory is buildable, plus a
numerical suite testing its postulates for internal consistency. The core argument:

1. **Attention is approximately conserved** (bounded supply, zero-sum reallocation,
   softmax-normalized in artificial analogues) → continuity equation; belief enters as
   a drift field on the flux, not a conserved stock.
2. **Reflexivity is solved at fixed points**: a forecast robust to publication is a
   mean-field game (Lasry-Lions) equilibrium of the prediction→reaction map.
3. **Correlated agents are handled by coarse-graining**: Simon near-decomposability →
   statistical unit is the community block, not the individual; N_eff ≈ K blocks.
4. **Engine** (§6 of paper): state-space system; stock-and-flow macro layer + per-block
   transport master equation on a topic graph + MFG belief closure; EnKF assimilation;
   ensemble forecasts; state-dependent skill horizon τ*.
5. **Failure modes**: (a) criticality — correlation length diverges, N_eff collapses,
   τ*→0; (b) misspecification — OOD agents (the Mule), unfixable by more data.
6. **Prediction–control duality**: diverging susceptibility kills prediction and
   maximizes control leverage at the same moment → regime-switching architecture.

## What to verify (priority order)

1. **Math audit of the paper.** Check: row-sum-zero rate matrix actually implies
   conservation of 1ᵀp in Eq. (master); the EnKF equation as written; whether the
   claimed mapping "forward FP equation = continuity equation of §3" is legitimate or
   hand-wavy; whether N_eff = K/(1+(K−1)r̄) is the right effective-sample-size formula.
2. **Reproduce sims.py end to end.** Confirm the printed numbers within seed tolerance.
3. **E3 slope discrepancy.** −0.63 vs theoretical −0.5. Our explanation: bistable
   well-hopping inflates small-K variance. Test this (e.g., theta=0 monostable case
   should give −0.5). If the explanation fails, flag it.
4. **E5 model fairness.** The reaction function is hand-tuned logistic. Check the
   fixed-point claims are robust across reasonable parameter ranges, not an artifact
   of the chosen constants (8, 4·0.4, −6g, −3).
5. **E4 N_eff metric.** Correlation-based N_eff only fell 64→27 while sign-synchrony
   hit 1.0. We claim corr-of-fluctuations is the wrong order parameter for well
   synchronization. Verify or refute; suggest the right estimator.
6. **Code hygiene.** sims.py contains a dead placeholder block in the first E4 sweep
   loop (vestigial `d=...` and `tau.append(rbar)` later overwritten) and the final
   E4 figure was produced by a separate inline script. Clean merge wanted.
7. **Paper claims vs sims.** The sims verify *internal consistency only* (the paper's
   own equations produce the claimed phenomena). Confirm the paper never overclaims
   empirical validation. §Limits has a falsifiability list — check the sims don't get
   cited as satisfying it.
8. **Factual spot-checks.** SVB running-example claims (§1 and threaded): high-level
   accuracy of the March 2023 sequence (community-concentrated run, Twitter
   acceleration, Sunday deposit guarantee). Citations: Lasry-Lions 2007, Simon 1962/
   1971, Airoldi 2008, Scheffer 2009, Pathak 2022 — verify all real and correctly
   attributed. Lethain/systems library attribution.

## Known weaknesses (don't let us hide them)

- The MFG "solved inside the forward model" closure (Eq. closure) is asserted, not
  demonstrated; no MFG solver exists in sims.py. E5 is a 1-D cartoon of the fixed-point
  idea, not an MFG computation.
- "Social reanalysis corpus" is proposed infrastructure, not existing.
- Block detection from real interaction graphs is assumed feasible at the relevant
  scale; not tested.
- The dual-use section is real: the control half of the duality is a manipulation
  spec. The governance question is flagged, unresolved.
